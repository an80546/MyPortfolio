# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors are teachers, reviewers, interviewers, and people browsing the owner's work from desktop or mobile devices. They need to quickly understand who Chen Yi-Jun is, what skills he is developing, and which projects demonstrate his frontend and UI/UX ability.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio presents personal background, skills, certifications, and project work in a way that is easy to scan and credible as a student frontend/UIUX portfolio. Success means a visitor can form a clear impression on mobile without horizontal scrolling, excessive reading fatigue, or hidden project context.

## Brand Personality

Showcase-focused, thoughtful, and memorable. The portfolio should feel more like a project gallery than a plain resume while keeping the content readable and sincere.

## Anti-references

Avoid a dense resume page, a generic template-like card wall, and mobile layouts that simply shrink the desktop page. Avoid hiding important project proof behind unclear interactions.

## Design Principles

- Lead with work and identity, then let details unfold.
- Keep mobile reading light by revealing long text only when visitors ask for it.
- Make project and skill cards easy to scan before asking for a tap.
- Preserve the existing blue-purple visual identity while giving mobile pages a stronger showcase rhythm.
- Favor clear touch targets, stable layouts, and readable Traditional Chinese text.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Mobile controls should be large enough for comfortable thumb use. Expanded content must remain accessible through native buttons with clear state text. Motion should not be required to understand content.
